# Niche Map — The Full Treasure-Hunter Universe

Every market where people hunt for undervalued items and need to know "what is it / what's it worth / where do I sell it." Each is a candidate god-tier app using the shared engine. Ranked by how strong the opportunity is for a solo builder.

Each niche note covers: the user, the wedge over Google/generic search, the key specialist data it needs, and the communities to launch in.

---

## TIER 1 — Strongest (validate these first)

### 🎨 Art (ArtPeriod) — BUILT
Estate/thrift art, unsigned & regional pieces. Wedge: Google Lens fails on non-famous art. Needs: attribution confidence, auction comps, stolen-art check, authentication routing. Communities: r/whatsthisworth, r/flipping, estate-sale FB groups, in-person at sales.

### 🪙 Coins (CoinGrader) — BUILT
Coin roll hunters, estate coin lots, inherited collections. Wedge: honest grade ranges + key-date/variety detection + cleaning flags. Sophisticated, loyal, reachable audience. Needs: Sheldon grading, mint marks, varieties, PCGS/NGC cert thresholds. Communities: r/coins, r/Numismatics, CoinTalk, coin shows, CRH Facebook groups. **Best second app — most concentrated, most active community.**

### 💎 Jewelry & Watches (e.g. "Hallmark")
Inherited jewelry boxes, estate lots, thrift jewelry. Wedge: hallmark decoding + real-vs-costume triage + "is this worth appraising." Higher dollar values per item. Needs: hallmark/purity marks, gem ID, maker signatures, melt vs collector value. Communities: r/jewelry, r/Watches, r/flipping, estate jewelry FB groups. **Best dollar-value-per-scan; honest positioning is "triage, then see an appraiser."**

## TIER 2 — Strong (clear demand, slightly harder)

### 🏺 Antiques & Furniture (e.g. "Provenance")
Furniture flippers, estate buyers. Wedge: period/maker ID + construction analysis (dovetails, secondary woods) + refinishing-kills-value honesty. Needs: maker marks, period/style, construction, originality. Communities: r/furniture, r/flipping, antique-mall communities, FB furniture-flip groups.

### 🫖 Pottery & Ceramics (e.g. "Maker's Mark")
Art pottery hunters. Wedge: bottom-mark ID + studio/artist attribution + chip-impact-on-value. Needs: maker marks, glaze lines, artist initials, condition. Communities: r/Pottery, r/ArtPottery, Replacements community, pottery collector FB groups.

### 🥈 Silver & Metals
Sterling/silverplate hunters. Wedge: hallmark decoding + melt-value floor + monogram devaluation. Needs: hallmarks, assay marks, troy-weight estimate, maker ID. Communities: r/Silverbugs, r/flipping, silver-stacking forums.

### 📚 Rare Books & Ephemera (e.g. "First Edition")
Book scouts, estate libraries. Wedge: edition-point identification + DJ condition + signed-copy detection. Needs: edition points, condition grade, dust jacket, inscriptions. Communities: r/RareBooks, r/BookCollecting, AbeBooks community, book-scout FB groups.

## TIER 3 — Viable (good but narrower or harder)

### ⚔️ Militaria & Medals
Wedge: named-medal premium + period-correct vs reproduction + legal flags. Sensitive category (legal/ethical care needed). Communities: militaria forums, OMSA, gun/militaria shows.

### 🎸 Vintage Instruments
Guitars, amps, brass. Wedge: serial-number dating + spec verification + mod-impact on value. Needs: serial decoding, model ID, originality. Communities: r/Guitar, Reverb community, gear forums. (Reverb already strong here — wedge must be the ID/triage, not the marketplace.)

### 🧸 Toys & Collectibles
Vintage toys, action figures, models. Wedge: variant ID + condition grading + completeness. Communities: r/ActionFigures, r/vintagetoys, collector FB groups.

### 👜 Designer Goods & Fashion
Thrifted designer bags/clothing. Wedge: authenticity triage (real vs counterfeit) + resale-value-by-platform. HIGH counterfeit-risk = position carefully as triage, not authentication. Communities: r/flipping, r/RepLadies (auth discussion), Poshmark/Depop communities.

### 💿 Vinyl Records
Wedge: pressing/matrix identification + condition grading + Discogs comps. Discogs is strong here — wedge is fast field ID. Communities: r/vinyl, r/VinylCollectors, Discogs.

### 📷 Vintage Cameras / Electronics
Wedge: model dating + working-vs-parts assessment + collector vs user value. Communities: r/AnalogCommunity, camera forums.

### 🪑 Mid-Century Modern (cross-cut of furniture)
Hot resale category. Could be its own app or a furniture sub-mode. Wedge: designer/manufacturer attribution. Communities: r/MidCenturyModern, Chairish community.

### 🏆 Sports Memorabilia & Trading Cards
Wedge: card ID + grading estimate (PSA/BGS) + comp pricing. Note: huge existing tools (PSA, 130point) — wedge must be field-speed triage. Communities: r/sportscards, r/baseballcards.

### 🔫 Antique Tools
Surprisingly strong reseller niche. Wedge: maker/age ID + collector-vs-user value. Communities: r/handtools, r/flipping, tool-collector forums.

## How to choose the next one

After ArtPeriod, the two best second apps are **Coins** (most concentrated, most active, most loyal community) and **Jewelry** (highest dollar value per item, motivated sellers). You've already started CoinGrader — finish validating it.

Pick the next niche by answering: *Which community can I reach most easily, and where is the "what's it worth" pain most intense and most answerable from a photo?* Let the question "do you also do X?" from real users point the way.
